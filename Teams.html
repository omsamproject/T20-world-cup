20 Teams Of ICC Men's T20 World Cup 2024:

India
Led by the experience of Rohit Sharma and Virat Kohli, India's balanced squad dominated the 2024 T20 World Cup. Their exceptional bowling attack, featuring Jasprit Bumrah and spin duo Chahal and Jadeja, stifled opponents, while their batting firepower secured an undefeated run to the championship title.
Australia
Led by the aggressive Mitchell Marsh, Australia brought a star-studded squad featuring power hitters like David Warner and Glenn Maxwell. Their explosive batting combined with a potent pace attack made them strong contenders. Despite a dominant group stage performance, they couldn't replicate their form in the knockout stages, falling short of reaching the finals.
England
The defending champions, England, entered the World Cup with a formidable batting order led by the likes of Joe Root and Jos Buttler. Their bowling attack, spearheaded by Jofra Archer and Adil Rashid, remained a force to be reckoned with. However, their reign as champions ended in the semifinals against a resurgent Indian side.
West Indies
The passionate home crowd provided a significant boost for the co-hosts, West Indies. Their batting lineup, featuring explosive players like Chris Gayle and Nicholas Pooran, often displayed destructive power. However, their bowling attack lacked consistency, resulting in an early group stage exit, much to the disappointment of their home fans.
South Africa
South Africa displayed unwavering consistency throughout the tournament. Their batting lineup, anchored by the experienced Quinton de Kock and the explosive Rassie van der Dussen, provided a solid foundation. Their bowling attack, boasting the likes of Kagiso Rabada and Anrich Nortje, was equally impressive.
New Zealand
Renowned for their strategic gameplay and strong bowling unit, New Zealand looked promising. Led by the ever-reliable Kane Williamson, their batting was composed and calculated. However, they couldn't translate their potential into deep tournament progress, bowing out after the Super 8 stage.
Pakistan
Pakistan's campaign was a tale of close encounters. Their group stage clash against arch-rivals India was a highly anticipated match, but they couldn't secure a win. Despite flashes of brilliance, they couldn't progress past the Super 8 stage, leaving their fans yearning for more.
Sri Lanka
A team with a rich cricketing heritage, Sri Lanka aimed for a strong comeback in the T20 World Cup. Led by the experienced Dinesh Chandimal, their batting displayed moments of brilliance. However, they couldn't find the consistency needed to advance beyond the Super 8 stage.
Bangladesh
A consistent performer in ICC events, Bangladesh brought a well-rounded squad with designs on upsetting the established powers. Their batting, featuring the likes of Shakib Al Hasan and Liton Das, promised solidity, while their bowling attack aimed to exploit weaknesses.
Afghanistan
Led by the wily spin wizard Rashid Khan, Afghanistan's strength resided in their world-class bowling attack. Their passionate unit, known for their never-say-die attitude, craved to cause a major upset during the tournament. Look out for explosive batting cameos from Mohammad Nabi or Hazratullah Zazai to complement their potent bowling.
Ireland
Ireland possessed a wealth of experience in global tournaments. Their batting lineup, featuring the big-hitting Paul Stirling, promised fireworks. Their bowling attack, with a mix of pace and spin, aimed to control the flow of runs and pick up crucial wickets.
Canada
Marking their historic T20 World Cup debut, Canada entered the fray with youthful exuberance and a hunger to prove themselves. Their enthusiastic squad, brimming with fresh talent like the big-hitting Navdeep Poonia, was determined to make a mark on the global stage.
Scotland
Scotland, an experienced associate nation, entered the tournament with a reputation for causing upsets against bigger teams. Their batting, anchored by the veteran Kyle Coetzer, boasted a blend of experience and explosiveness. Their bowling attack, known for their accuracy and swing, looked to exploit chinks in the opposition's armor.
Namibia
Renowned for their fighting spirit and upsets in previous tournaments, Namibia entered the fray with a team known for their never-say-die attitude. Their batting, featuring the explosive David Wiese, could take the attack to any bowling lineup. Their bowling attack, spearheaded by the pace of Ruben Trumpelmann, aimed to restrict runs and create chaos with their variations.
Netherlands
The Netherlands, another team known for causing upsets, looked to repeat their heroics. Their batting boasted experienced players like Stephan Myburgh, capable of anchoring the innings or going big. Their bowling attack, with a mix of pace and spin led by the veteran Pieter Seelaar, aimed to be economical and exploit weaknesses in the opposition.
Uganda
Uganda also made their World Cup debut, eager to showcase their cricketing prowess. Their batting relied on the power-hitting of Ronak Patel, while their bowling, spearheaded by the wily Frank Nsubuga, aimed to restrict runs and create breakthroughs.
Nepal
Debuting in the World Cup, Nepal represented the next generation of cricketing talent from the Himalayas. Led by the experienced Gyanendra Malla, their batting focused on calculated strokes and smart running between the wickets. Their bowling attack, featuring the young spinner Sandeep Lamichhane, aimed to surprise with their variations.
USA
The USA also made their T20 World Cup debut, bringing a wave of enthusiasm. Their uncapped players, like the promising young pace bowler Sushant Singh, aimed to learn from the experience while showcasing their skills against established teams.
Oman
Oman, a participant in the qualifiers, brought a touch of the unexpected to the tournament. Their batting, featuring the swashbuckling Aaqib Ilyas, could take the attack to any bowling lineup. Their bowling attack, with a strong spin contingent led by Khawar Ali, aimed to deceive batsmen and control the run rate.
